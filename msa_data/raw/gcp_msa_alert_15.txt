Subject: [Legal Notice] Action Required: Deprecation of Legacy Access Control Lists (ACLs) for Cloud Storage
From: The Google Cloud Team
Date: February 18, 2026

You are receiving this communication because your organization has Google Cloud Storage buckets utilizing Legacy Access Control Lists (ACLs) rather than uniform bucket-level access.

WHAT YOU NEED TO KNOW
To improve security, simplify permissions, and align with the Cloud Data Processing Addendum (CDPA), Google Cloud is migrating all remaining Cloud Storage buckets to use Identity and Access Management (IAM) exclusively.

Between May 07, 2026 and May 28, 2026, Google Cloud will forcefully enable "Uniform bucket-level access" on all buckets listed in your affected projects. 

Key Changes:
* Any granular, object-level ACLs currently applied to individual files within your buckets will be permanently disabled and ignored.
* Access will be governed strictly by bucket-level or project-level IAM roles.

WHAT YOU NEED TO DO
Action advised before May 07, 2026:
1. Review the IAM permissions on your affected buckets.
2. Ensure that any service accounts, users, or external services that rely on object-level ACLs have been granted equivalent roles (e.g., `roles/storage.objectViewer`) at the bucket level.
3. Enable "Uniform bucket-level access" manually ahead of the deadline to test your application's behavior and ensure no disruption to your services.
